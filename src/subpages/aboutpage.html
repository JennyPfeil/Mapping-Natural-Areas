Natural Areas Mapping Project

About the Project

The Wayfinding Project, a part of the Trinity Sustainability Initiative, is a project by and for students at the University of Toronto. The project is dedicated to make Toronto’s natural areas more approachable, through our map, website content, and various community-building initiatives.

We aim to have a student-centred approach that gives students the agency to feel informed and comfortable approaching nature. This means including campus-specific greenspaces, addressing student concerns directly, and providing information on access needs such as transportation, study spaces, and facilities available at the greenspaces.

To use the map, simply select filters that are relevant to your needs and discover greenspaces throughout the GTA that address your criteria. You can find more information on each space by navigating to the webpage linked to its location on the map.

Our data is collected primarily through student volunteers that have explored greenspaces in their area. We have held Urban Nature Blitzes and surveys to facilitate this data collection. For the future, we hope to promote and create student initiatives, foster community through nature on campus, and connect students with their local natural environment.
